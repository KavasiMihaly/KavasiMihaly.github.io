When we talk about Self-Service BI, everyone has a different picture in their mind. Some it means complete freedom, others in means a safe "playground" for experimentation or just the ability interact with an existing report in a secure way. There are no one size fits all and there are 100s of different variations of self-service you can achieve.

Just for context: I started writing the blog post a while ago but some life changing events shifted my priorities for a while. Since then Fabric came out, and I think made this topic even more relevant than ever.

As nowdays everyone I wanted to understand how AI would summarise what we mean by Self-Service BI

What is Self-Service BI?

Self-service BI puts the power of data analysis directly into the hands of business users. It allows non-technical users to access, analyze, and visualize data without relying on IT or data specialists. With intuitive tools and user-friendly interfaces, self-service BI platforms enable users to explore data, create reports, and gain insights on their own terms.

ChatGPT

ChatGPT's definition is correct but quite vague and does not show the vast differences of individuals unique experiences and expectations.

When I asked the question "What is Self-Service BI mean to you?" during various projects, here are some of the answers I got:

  • "Prepared, linked data sources"

  • "Single source of truth"

  • "Automation"

  • "Customization of Reports"

  • "Exploration, experimentation"

  • "Security, trust"

  • "Ease of use"

These are different answers that come from different experiences and expectations. They are also contradicting each other is some cases. Like giving access to prepared data sources or the ability to personalize visuals on an existing report are on the two ends of the analytics process.

These discussions started in two main ways. Either there was complete freedom previously, leading to unmanageable chaos, and this needed to change. The other when tight controls were introduced early on but has not been managed, leaving businesses unhappy and prompting them to create their own unofficial BI solutions.

As I worked with these teams and organizations, I started to define the main levels of self-service in Power BI that are the most relevant.

In this picture you see the various levels. The blue pyramid represents the participation of a central BI/IT team.

This representation tries to show that a central team alone can only fulfill smaller and smaller slice of the overall need as we move up in the self-service levels. This is one of the reasons Self-Service BI tools appeared at all. If the BI teams were able to support most of the needs of business than there would not be a need for these.

Levels and what Self-Service means in them

Analytics

This is the most obvious level. Data Analysts, Decision Makers interact with the reports created for them. Cental BI/IT team is not involved in this level most of the time. To maximise the ability for consumers to self-serve themselves the report need to be created with this in mind. Also, we need to think about what we could do to provide support especially for new users of Power BI.

Self-Service means:

  • Setting Slicers/Filters

  • Clicking on visuals

  • Drilldown/Drill through

  • Export data

  • Subscriptions and Alerts

  • Sharing

  • Collaboration, Embedding (Teams, PowerPoint)

  • Personal Bookmarks for Storytelling

  • Personalized visuals

Benefit of Self-Service:

  • Set your own context

  • Ability to dig into details

  • Get answers for many questions from one Report (avoid the need to create multiple reports or presentations)

Reporting/Data Discovery

This level is about data discovery or creating reports for others out of an existing dataset. Again, in order to maximise the value that can be extracted from the data and make report creation simple, the dataset need to be designed in a certain way. You can read more about it in this previous blog post:

Self-Service means:

  • Self-service data discovery

  • Ad hoc reporting

  • Designing user journey

  • Creating report specific calculations

  • Report design

  • Creating end-user support documentation

  • Deciding which report features should be enabled to consumers

  • Accessibility considerations

Benefit of Self-Service:

  • Find insights from the data

  • Extract all the value that is in the dataset (Decreases the need to create additional data models for information already covered in the dataset)

Risk of Self-Service (and Mitigation): we have to consider the risk of enabling self-service at this level without proper governance process and audit information.

  • Report proliferation, huge number of unused reports (report lifecycle management) and users cannot decide which report to use (certification standards)

  • Higher chance of incorrect insights (self-service data model design, training, and support for the business community)

Data Modelling

At this level, the business can create their own data models based on the available prepared data sources. This enables business to reuse data for different purposes that requires different ways to model the data. Requires higher level of technical knowledge, therefore proper training and support need to be provided to business.

Self-Service means:

  • Selecting data sources

  • Combining, prefiltering data

  • Data model design

  • Creating relationships

  • Creating calculations

  • Configuring data refresh

  • Performance management

Benefit of Self-Service:

  • Set context

  • Ability to dig into details

  • Answers for many questions with one Report

Risk of Self-Service:

  • Incorrectly modeled data leading to false insights.

  • Non-performant, high-cost data models

Data Preparation/Data Engineering

Basically, at this level the business users can build end to end solutions. Most risky to allow self-service at this level because issues here will spiral through the subsequent layer in the analytics process.

Self-Service means:

  • Evaluating new data sources

  • Creating data pipelines

  • Cleaning and aligning data

  • Managing data quality issues

Benefit of Self-Service:

  • Set context

  • Ability to dig into details

  • Answers for many questions with one Report

Risk of Self-Service:

  • Requires the most technical and data knowledge

  • If proper guardrails are not in place managing the created solution becomes a headache (constantly failing pipelines)

  • Misaligned data source

Define what you mean by Self-Service

As you saw there are many options to self-service. It is beneficial for an organization to define what they mean by Self-Service BI for the different user personas they should define in their governance framework.

Enablig self-service can make huge progress to data-driven decision making but also introduces additional risks that need to be managed by a proper governance framework and report usage insights.
